Home

Hello! I am Phaneendra Pydimarri, a PhD graduate from McMaster University, Canada. My passion lies in continuous learning, and my areas of interests include:

  • Quantum Computing & Information Theory
  • Quantum Communications
  • Mathematical Ideas and Implementation in Signal and Image Processing
  • Embedded Systems and Communication

My goal is to become a competent and versatile researcher/developer. I am currently working as a Postdoctoral Researcher at TiHAN, Indian Institute of Technology, Hyderabad, India (Jul 2023 - Present). I am involved with the Technology Innovation Hub on Autonomous Navigation (TiHAN), funded by the Government of India. My work focuses on 5G/6G cellular-based vehicular communication (connected vehicles), aiming for low-latency communication and data transfer using mounted sensors on vehicles, with the ultimate goal of quantum sensing, navigation, and communication. I am currently working as a team lead in connected vehicles technology project at TiHAN.

Previously, I have worked on use case development in classical connected vehicle technology, including:

  • Vehicle-to-Vehicle Communication: Blind Spot Detection Warning, Lane Change Warning, Emergency Brake Warning
  • Vehicle-to-Cloud Communication: Dynamical Offloading Data to Cloud from Vehicle in Real Time

Projects

  • Signal Detection in Passive Sonar (2017): Investigated the principles of passive sonar detection by studying the modeling and processing of the output signal of the FFT beamformer.
  • Analysis of Geometry of Diffusion Tensor for Scattering Using Stochastic Calculus (2016): Analyzed the geometrical structure and behavior of K-scattering waves via numerical solutions to the associated stochastic differential equations.
  • Estimation of Target Cross Section under Non-Gaussian Noise Using Stochastic Calculus (2016): Used stochastic calculus to analyze and estimate target cross sections under non-Gaussian noise conditions, providing an analytical expression for the estimate.
  • Image Denoising Using Partial Differential Equations (2016): Studied various methods of denoising images under additive Gaussian noise and implemented a partial differential equation approach, comparing the results to other methods.
